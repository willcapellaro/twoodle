The Importance-Urgency Matrix is a boardroom classic. It serves a simple but effective way to manage tasks based on key criteria: what things are important and how pressing they are. It is sometimes called the Eisenhower Decision Matrix based on an efficiency-obsessed hobby farmer that popularized the recipe. Use it for task management when demands differ.

Why it works

The purpose of an Importance-Urgency Analysis is to help you identify the activities that you should prioritize and the ones you should ignore. By challenging instinctive responses, you regain control over your environment and external demands instead of being controlled by them. You can free up time by eliminating activities that have minimal value for long-term goals.

There is no need to undergo specialized training in order to create an Importance-Urgency Matrix, and the opportunity costs will be minimal, except for your time.

When it doesn't work

Importance-Urgency doesn't take into account other criteria, such as ease of effort or appropriateness. If tasks have a wide range of difficulty, some taking hours and some requiring days or more, it's recommended to try the Impact-Ease matrix. If a variety of skills or strengths are required for a multitude of tasks, try Impact-Specialization (coming soon).

Avoid imitation recipes! Urgency-Importance sounds similar, but it is the devil's work. Kidding not kidding. Just be aware that the blog currently doesn't support swapping the X and Y axis.

Recipe in Practice

Interact with some content to learn this recipe.

To start, add a few items that are currently vying for your time:



Prioritization

Now that you have your items, sort them based on their importance. Use drag and drop to sort your items. The top item should be the most important. Ignore timing for this exercise, think only of the value delivered by completing each task.



Now add the timing factor by sorting the items one more time. Only think of the clock and calendar here; Importance isn't important here. Think only of when each task needs to be completed. The first item should be utmost, last one chillaxed.



Twoodle Results

Finally, reap the benefits of your work. Because you're doing an Importance-Urgency twoodle, highest priority items are in the top right quad. Top left and bottom right are also of value. It's recommended to avoid those in the bottom left.
